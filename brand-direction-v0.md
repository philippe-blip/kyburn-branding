# Brand Direction — v0 (March 2025)

## Core Concept
**"Cybernetics is back."**

Cybernetics — the science of control, feedback, and communication in systems — was the intellectual foundation of the 20th century's automation dreams. It was abandoned when computing became digital and siloed. Now, with AI agents, feedback loops, and autonomous systems, it's quietly returning. Kyburn is the agency that *builds* that future.

## Why This Works
- Differentiates from every other "AI automation agency" with a generic tech aesthetic
- Gives permission for intellectual depth in content (rare in SME-facing agencies)
- The name "Kyburn" carries it — sounds like it belongs in this universe
- Neo-cyberpunk is visually distinctive without being alienating to business owners
- Content moat: rehash classic cybernetics ideas through the lens of modern tools

## The Intellectual Angle (Content)
Classic cybernetics thinkers to mine:
- **Norbert Wiener** — *Cybernetics* (1948), feedback loops, the human use of human beings
- **Stafford Beer** — Viable System Model, management cybernetics (directly applicable to SMEs)
- **Ashby's Law of Requisite Variety** — a system must have as much variety as its environment (AI as variety amplifier)
- **Gregory Bateson** — information and pattern
- **Heinz von Foerster** — second-order cybernetics, self-organizing systems

Content format: "Wiener described [X] in 1948. Here's what it looks like with n8n in 2025."

## Visual Direction
**Neo-cyberpunk** — not dystopian/gritty, but *optimistic cyberpunk*. The aesthetic of possibility, not decay.

References to gather:
- Current kyburn.com already uses dark + cyan — extend this, don't abandon it
- Jaap's cyberpunk profile (v1) is a starting point
- References: Ghost in the Shell (original), Tron Legacy palette, Synthwave, Figma's dark mode era
- Typography: geometric sans (not neon cursive — that's retro, not neo)
- Key visual element: feedback loop / circular flow as a motif

Tone: **Precise. Confident. A little philosophical. Not trying to be cool — just right.**

## Name
**Dropping "Labs"** — it signals experimentation and R&D, which contradicts the core promise: we do the work that needs doing *now*.

**Name candidates** (TBD):
- **Kyburn** — clean, ownable, works standalone
- **Kyburn AI** — adds clarity, slightly more generic

"Kyburn" etymology to play with:
- Ky = Kybernetes (Greek: steersman, governor) → origin of "cybernetics"
- Burn = transformation, intensity, execution speed — we don't consult, we ignite change
- Possible tagline territory: "Steer faster." / "We do the work." / "Your workflows, transformed."

The name already does the heavy lifting — "Labs" was pulling in the wrong direction.

## How This Affects the Website
- **Homepage**: Lead with the narrative, not just the services
- **Blog/Content**: Cybernetics canon → modern application (SEO + authority)
- **About**: The mission is bigger than "we build automations" — frame around the revival
- **Visual system**: Consistent dark + accent color, geometric, precise spacing

## Inspiration Thread — The Culture Series (Iain M. Banks)
**Added**: March 2026

The Culture is Banks' post-scarcity civilization: technology handles everything, and humans are free to pursue whatever they want — adventure, pleasure, philosophy, art. No one manages in the old sense. They *direct*, at the speed of thought, from wherever they are.

**The angle for Kyburn**: the promise isn't efficiency — it's *freedom*. The end state is a business owner on a mountain or a beach, talking into their phone, making decisions in real time, the machine handling the rest. That's not a productivity pitch — it's a lifestyle pitch backed by operational reality.

**Key motifs to explore**:
- **Post-scarcity operations** — you're not saving hours, you're buying back your life
- **Minds** (the Culture's AIs) — vast intelligence running quietly in the background, humans remain in control of direction
- **Orbital luxury** — abundance as the default state, not the reward
- **Speed of thought** — the gap between intention and execution collapses
- **Nomadic by design** — the business runs whether you're in Chiang Mai, the Azores, or a co-working space in Tbilisi

**Visual / tonal implications**:
- Less "grinding machine room", more "terrace overlooking the ocean, phone in hand"
- The neo-cyberpunk darkness can coexist: it's the *infrastructure* layer (precise, technical, invisible) — the Culture layer is the *life* layer (open, abundant, light)
- Possible tension to exploit: the machines are cyberpunk / the humans live in light
- Reference: the luxury of the Culture isn't gold and marble — it's *space, time, and effortlessness*

**How it connects to the Kyburn narrative**:
> "The richest people today have a human assistant who executes on a voice message. Tomorrow, every business owner will have a machine that does the same — but faster, 24/7, and at a fraction of the cost. We build that."
> *(Philippe's vision from Notion)*

The Culture is what the destination looks like. Cybernetics is the operating principle underneath.

**Status**: rough inspiration, not yet integrated into visual direction. Needs pairing with real reference images. Check: does this layer *onto* neo-cyberpunk or does it replace it? Probably two-layer solution.

## Inspiration Ref — Anima (anima-cc.com)
**Added**: March 2026 | **URL**: https://www.anima-cc.com/

Cardiac coherence mobile app. Mobile-only, immersive scroll experience.

**What's interesting for Kyburn**:
- **Arch + circle as primary shape language** — images live inside circular/arch containers that morph as you scroll. Not just decorative: the shapes *are* the motion. Could map to Kyburn's feedback loop / circular flow motif.
- **Deep muted teal as the brand color** — not far from where Kyburn's dark + cyan already sits. Their teal is warmer, more organic. Shows how teal can feel premium and calm rather than cold/tech.
- **Coral/salmon as the accent** — punchy against the dark teal. Warm highlight that feels human, not neon. Contrast to the neo-cyberpunk neon palette — worth considering as a softer alternative.
- **Atmospheric landscape photography** inside geometric frames — gives it depth and nature without being a "wellness cliché". The portal framing elevates the photography.
- **Scroll-driven morphing shapes** — circle → ring → arch as you scroll. Highly immersive without relying on 3D or particle effects. Achievable, memorable.
- **Minimal text, maximum atmosphere** — very few words per screen. Forces sharp copy. Aligns with Kyburn's "precise, confident" tone.

**What doesn't transfer**:
- Mobile-only is intentional for their product (app download). Kyburn needs desktop-first.
- The wellness/nature mood is too calm — Kyburn needs more edge, more precision.
- The coral accent might be too soft for the Kyburn positioning.

**Takeaways**:
1. The arch/portal shape as a recurring motif — could work as Kyburn's visual system element
2. Morphing scroll-driven shapes as interaction pattern (not just parallax)
3. Deep teal + warm accent as an alternative to dark + cyan if the palette evolves

## Inspiration Ref — Odoo Web Design (design.odoo.com)
**Added**: March 2026 | **URL**: https://design.odoo.com/fr_BE/services/web-design

French-language agency/services site with a striking dark aesthetic.

**Signals**:
- **Pure black + star/grain texture** — deep space feel without being gimmicky. The grain adds tactility and stops it feeling flat.
- **Lime/yellow-green accent** — used very sparingly (nav underline only). Pops hard against the black. Entirely different energy from cyan but same structural role.
- **Rounded rect images floating on black** — photos feel like objects in space, not embedded in layouts. Creates depth.
- **White + italic serif mix** — section labels in regular weight, headlines mixing serif and sans. Adds editorial quality.
- **Content-first dark** — not trying to be edgy, just premium and focused. Closer to Kyburn's "precise, confident" tone than neo-cyberpunk.

**What transfers**: The star/grain texture idea. The floating image treatment. The minimal accent used for punctuation only.
**What doesn't**: The overall aesthetic is still fairly conventional dark-agency. No memorable shape language or interaction.

## Inspiration Ref — Primland Residences (ownprimland.com)
**Added**: March 2026 | **URL**: https://ownprimland.com/

Luxury mountain residential community in Virginia. Ultra-premium real estate.

**Signals**:
- **Cream/off-white as the dominant background** — not white, not beige. A warm, aged-paper tone that feels expensive and calm. Complete opposite of dark-mode.
- **Deep forest green as brand color** — used in typography and image accents. Organic, premium, not tech. Interesting counter-reference if Kyburn ever goes light mode.
- **Massive centered serif typography** — editorial, slow, confident. "Among Virginia's Blue Ridge mountains, a once in a generation…" — the copy is as slow and deliberate as the layout.
- **Ghost botanical illustrations** — very faint leaf/branch motifs overlaid on content areas. Adds texture without cluttering. Could be interesting as a subtle background treatment.
- **Split layout: text left, full-bleed photo right** — classic but executed with extreme refinement. Images have no containers, no borders, they just *are*.
- **ALL CAPS section labels** — tight tracking, small, used as category markers. Elegant wayfinding.
- **The hero** — loading with soft animated type over a muted landscape. The italic "Beneath" paired with the roman "The Stars" — deliberate typographic contrast.

**What transfers for Kyburn**:
- The ghost/watermark illustration concept — could work with a circuit/feedback loop motif instead of botanicals
- The typography contrast (italic serif + roman) as a headline pattern
- The off-white tone as an option for the light sections if Kyburn has a two-tone layout
- The "slow, confident" pacing — resists the urge to cram information

**What doesn't**: Too organic/nature/luxury — not technical enough for Kyburn. The green is too "mountain resort."
